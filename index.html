About Me

I am broadly interested in the evolution of social systems and cognition, with a particular focus on language. I have collaborated with linguists, psychologists, complex systems scientists, and anthropologists to study issues surrounding the emergence, evolution, and dynamics of human language.

I am currently a British Academy Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Edinburgh, where I am studying the emergence of language using an online experimental game, in addition to agent based models and corpus data. I was formerly a postdoctoral researcher in the Social Dynamics Group based at the Institute for Scientific Interchange in Turin, Italy from 2014-2015, and the University of Rome La Sapienza from 2013-2014.

Research Interests:

  • The emergence and dynamics of shared conventions
  • The role of interaction in grounding and establishing shared communication systems
  • The relationship between population (social and demographic) structure and linguistic structure
  • The potential for shared sensory biases to underlie sound symbolism and contribute to lexical structure

To learn more about my research, see my projects and publications.

Projects

Cross-modality
Cross-modality
We all share the ability to make similar cross-modal associations: - connections between the senses. For example, most people think a high pitch is more light than dark. This ability underlies linguistic iconicity, which likely played a crucial role in language evolution, and has important implications for language acquisition.
Collaborators: Jools Simner, Simon Kirby, Mark Dingemanse, Tessa M. van Leeuwen
Rule Dynamics
Rule Dynamics
All languages have rules, but almost all rules have exceptions. How do exceptions to rules arise, or why do they survive? How is this affected by learner biases and population structure.
Collaborators: Vittorio Loreto, Claudio Castellano, Francesca Colaiori, Francesca Tria
Experimental Games
Experimental Games
In the last few decades, research in cognitive science has moved online, using platforms like Mechanical Turk and CrowdFlower. Science can also be crowd sourced through play - we're working on a large-scale game framework to study the emergence of cultural conventions.
Collaborators: Simon Kirby, Vittorio Loreto, Pietro Gravino, Bernardo Monechi
